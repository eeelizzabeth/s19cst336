Who?

John Backus was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, in 1924.

He received an M.S. in mathematics from Columbia University (1950).

Backus interviewed at IBM and was hired to work on programming their

new computer systems.By 1953, he was leading a small team to create

an easy-to-use programming language for scientific users.



What?

FORTRAN was the world's first high-level programming language.

It was developed at IBM by a small team led by John Backus.

The earliest version of FORTRAN was released in 1957 as a

programming tool for the IBM 704. Fifty years later, FORTRAN

remains the programming language of choice for large-scale

numerical calculations in science and engineering.
